Solitaire — The Meridian Cut

A single stone, set to catch only one hour of light.

Cut over eleven months by the House atelier, the Meridian departs from symmetry in favor of a single true facet — oriented, at the collector's request, to align with a specific latitude at dawn. No second stone will ever be cut to this angle.

Philosophy

What We Hold True

The principles that govern every admission into the Archive.
I.

Rarity is a Discipline

We refuse repetition as a matter of practice, not marketing. A design, once realized, is retired from possibility. This is what separates a creation from a product.

II.

Provenance is Permanent

Every work leaves the atelier with a documented history — materials, maker, date of admission. That record follows the piece for as long as it exists.

III.

Legacy Outlives Trend

We do not design for a season. A GIRI & CO. piece is built to be passed forward — to a collection, an heir, or an archive not yet imagined.
